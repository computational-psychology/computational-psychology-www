Prof. Dr. Marianne Maertens

Principal Investigator. My research bridges the areas of computer science and psychology. My key competencies are in experimental research methods and the study of human vision.

Dr. Guillermo Aguilar

Post-doc. I study visual perception in humans. I'm particularly interested in our perception of lightness, depth and transparency. I'm also interested in the evaluation and development of psychophysical methods that can reliably measure appearance.

Dr. Joris Vincent

Post-doc. I study visual perception in humans. I'm particularly interested in the perceptual representation of chromatic information in natural scenes. Ideally, I am looking to develop an (image computable) model for transforming chromaticity information to a representation that more accurately reflects human color perception.

Lynn Schmittwilken

Ph.D. student. I study visual processing in humans. In particular, I am interested in how we perceive the three-dimensional nature of our surrounds, and how this information could be used to improve computer vision models.
